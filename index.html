About

Iannetti Studies is an independent research project led by Ashley Iannetti, focused on identifying structural and mathematical constraints that recur across symbolic, cyclic, time-based, and biological signaling systems. Iannetti Studies serves as the umbrella research program under which the Rosetta Wheel™ Harmonics framework and related investigations are developed.

All research conducted under Iannetti Studies is organized within the Rosetta Wheel™ Harmonics framework — a protected, cross-domain structural framework used to evaluate recurring harmonic, geometric, and fractional organization across diverse domains. The framework is methodological and constraint-based, and is not presented as an interpretive or cultural-meaning model.

Analyses are evaluated through transformation invariance, null comparison, survivorship under bounded perturbation, and quantitative robustness testing. Claims are limited to what persists under these controls.

Research Areas:
Harmonic mathematics · cyclic structures · symbolic sequence analysis · calendrical systems · pattern theory · biological signaling constraints
